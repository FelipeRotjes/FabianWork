Carlos Fabián’s theatre experience includes roles as artistic director, assistant director, stage manager, teacher, designer, producer and actor in more than 30 productions. In his home town Caracas, he studied with directors: Orlando Arocha, Diana Volpe, Guillermo Díaz Yuma, Miguel Issa, Carlos Sánchez Torrealba and María Fernanda Ferro.


His directorial debut with the play “Tom At The Farm” by Canadian playwright Michel Marc Bouchard, was the winner of first place in the III Festival of Young Director of Trasnocho’s Cultural Foundation in 2017.


In 2020, he co-directed alongside actress Mónica Quintero the work-in-progress “De: Circe” for the first online international festival of monologues by Waterpeople Theater based in Chicago. Fabián is the artistic director of the project “LITTLE SPACES”, an outdoors theatrical performance project that uses real testimonies to explore relevant topics about the city and its individuals, was one of Knight Foundation’s “KNIGHT NEW WORK 2020” winning project ideas. Most recently, he has been working with the Miami Beach-based theater company “Miami New Drama” as a teacher in the MIND THEATER WORKSHOP programs for the young community in south Florida.
